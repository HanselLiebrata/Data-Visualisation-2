EVOLUTION OF INTERNET ACCESSIBILITY AND USAGE (1980-2020)

Four Decades of Digital Transformation: From Novelty to Necessity

Over the past four decades, the internet has transformed from a novelty to an essential tool for communication, work, education, and entertainment. This choropleth map visualizes the growth and spread of internet usage across different countries from 1980 to 2020.


The choropleth visualization, based on 2020 data, provides a revealing perspective on global internet adoption patterns. Developed nations continue to exhibit high rates of internet usage, a testament to their advanced digital infrastructure and accessibility. However, stark disparities are evident, particularly in specific regions of Africa and Asia. These disparities emphasize significant opportunities for growth and the pressing need for infrastructural enhancements in these areas. This persistent digital divide, as presented by the 2020 data, underscores the imperative for concerted global initiatives aimed at bridging these gaps and ensuring equitable internet access for all.


Historical Evolution


The historical evolution of internet usage from 1980-2020 offers a compelling narrative on global digital transformation. Initially, the rates of internet adoption were modest across all regions, reflecting the nascent stage of this technology. However, the turn of the century marked a pronounced surge, especially for Europe and the Americas, aligning with rapid technological advancements and increased accessibility. Asia and Oceania too observed substantial growth, albeit at a slightly deferred pace. Conversely, Africa's trajectory, while displaying a positive incline in recent years, indicates a lag, emphasizing the challenges of infrastructural development and accessibility in the continent. The trajectories underscore the transformative impact of the internet over four decades while also highlighting the urgency to address regional disparities.


The multi-line chart offers a contrasting perspective on the global average of broadband and cellular subscriptions from 1980 to 2020. Broadband subscriptions display a meteoric rise starting from the early 2000s, peaking close to universal adoption. This surge mirrors the global push towards high-speed internet connectivity, emphasizing its role in socio-economic activities and digital inclusivity. In comparison, cellular subscriptions showcase a more gradual and steady ascent, maintaining relevance but not achieving the same ubiquity as broadband. The trajectories shed light on global preferences and accessibility, underscoring the strategic importance of broadband as a primary means of digital communication and its potential implications for global connectivity strategies in the future.


Today's Mobile & Broadband Reach by Region


Drawing from the latest 2020 data and in comparison to our previous analysis on mobile subscriptions, Europe not only leads in cellular connectivity but also dominates in regional broadband subscription rates. The average broadband subscription in Europe is markedly higher than other regions, showcasing its advanced digital infrastructure. On the other end, Africa, while having a significant mobile reach as seen in the pie chart, lags in broadband connectivity. This juxtaposition between mobile and broadband data suggests that while mobile connectivity might be widespread, the availability or affordability of broadband services is still a challenge in certain regions. The contrast between Europe and Africa's broadband subscription rates underscores a persisting digital divide, emphasizing the need for increased infrastructural development and potential policy interventions.


In the "Rings of Connection" pie chart illustrating regional mobile subscriptions per 100 people for the year 2020, the spread across continents offers a clear snapshot of mobile connectivity.
